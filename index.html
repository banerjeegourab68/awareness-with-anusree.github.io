Child Counseling Tips

Use play therapy—let children express feelings through games and art.
Maintain simple, clear language and concrete examples.
Build trust by allowing them to lead conversations at their own pace.
Validate emotions: acknowledge “You seem sad,” rather than dismissing feelings.
Involve caregivers via periodic check-ins to reinforce progress at home.

Parent Counseling Tips

Practice active listening: mirror back what you hear before responding.
Encourage “I” statements to reduce blame: “I feel overwhelmed when…”
Set realistic, shared goals for family routines and discipline strategies.
Teach stress-management techniques: deep breathing and brief mindfulness breaks.
Offer empathy: normalize parental challenges and reinforce strengths.

Adult Counseling Tips

Frame open-ended questions: “What’s happening for you today?”
Encourage self-reflection through journaling prompts after each session.
Use cognitive restructuring: identify and challenge unhelpful thoughts.
Suggest activity scheduling to counteract avoidance and boost mood.
Foster social support by exploring trustworthy connections and boundaries.

Depression Support Tips

Introduce behavioral activation: encourage small, achievable daily tasks.
Monitor thought records: track automatic negative thoughts and counter-evidence.
Recommend sleep hygiene: consistent bedtime and reduced screen time before sleep.
Promote physical activity: even a 10-minute walk can boost endorphins.
Check for safety: ask directly about self-harm and have crisis resources ready.
